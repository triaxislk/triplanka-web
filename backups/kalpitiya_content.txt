                <h2>Kalpitiya: The Untamed Peninsula</h2>
                <div class="underline"></div>
            </div>
            <div class="about-grid">
                <div class="about-text">
                    <p>Kalpitiya is a stunning coastal peninsula on the northwestern tip of Sri Lanka, known for its raw natural beauty and world-class adventure sports. It's a sanctuary for those seeking a serene escape away from the developed tourist trails.</p>
                    <p>The region is world-renowned for <strong>kitesurfing</strong>, with the Kalpitiya Lagoon offering perfect wind conditions. Beyond the adrenaline, it is a premier spot for <strong>dolphin and whale watching</strong>, with massive pods frequenting the offshore waters.</p>
                </div>

                <h2>Puttalam: Salt, Lagoons, and Legends</h2>
                <div class="underline"></div>
            </div>
            <div class="about-grid">
                <div class="about-image">
                    <img src="../../Images/Site Photos/DSC_0145.jpg" alt="Puttalam Lagoon" style="border-radius: 20px; box-shadow: var(--shadow);">
                </div>
                <div class="about-text">
                    <p>Puttalam is a region of vast salt pans and tranquil lagoons. It holds a legendary place in history as the landing site of Prince Vijaya, the founder of the Sinhalese race. Today, it remains a vital hub for the island's salt and fishing industries.</p>
                    <p>Nature lovers can explore the <strong>Wilpattu National Park</strong>, Sri Lanka's largest and oldest wildlife reserve, famous for its leopards and unique "Willu" (natural lakes).</p>
                </div>
            </div>
