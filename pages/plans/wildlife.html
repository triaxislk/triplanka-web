Wildlife Lovers Signature Itinerary

Expertly crafted 18-day journey for nature enthusiasts

Back to Journey Hub

Wildlife Exploration Spotlight

For a perfect wildlife adventure, keep these local safety rules in mind:

Wildlife Respect

Never exit the jeep in national parks. Do not feed or provoke wild animals.

Remote Safety

Be cautious near lakes or "Willus"; crocodiles can be present even in shallow water.

Silent Shutter

Keep noise levels low and avoid flash photography to prevent startling animals.

Sri Lanka is a premier destination for wildlife enthusiasts due to its rich biodiversity and a variety of landscapes. A well-planned wildlife voyage can offer the chance to see elephants, leopards, sloth bears, blue whales, and a plethora of bird species. Heres a comprehensive 18-day signature itinerary to explore the islands most iconic wildlife spots:

Duration: 18 Days

Day 1-2: Arrival and Wilpattu National Park

  • Arrive at Colombo and transfer to Wilpattu.
  • Wilpattu is famous for its unique natural lakes and as a top spot for leopard sightings.


Day 3-4: Anuradhapura and Minneriya National Park

  • Visit the ancient city of Anuradhapura.
  • Safari at Minneriya, home to the famous Elephant Gathering.


Day 5: Sigiriya Rock and Habarana

  • Climb Sigiriya Rock Fortress.
  • Enjoy birdwatching in Habarana.


Day 6-7: Kandy and Forest Reserves

  • Explore Udawattakele Forest Reserve for endemic birds.
  • Nature walk in Royal Botanical Gardens.


Day 8-9: Knuckles Mountain Range

  • Trek through dense forests and grasslands in the UNESCO-listed Knuckles range.


Day 10-11: Horton Plains & Nuwara Eliya

  • Guided trek to Worlds End and Bakers Falls.


Day 12-13: Yala National Park

  • Full-day jeep safari for leopards and sloth bears.


Day 14: Bundala National Park

  • Explore the Ramsar Wetland, a magnet for migratory birds like flamingos.


Day 15: Mirissa Whale Watching

  • Morning whale-watching tour for blue whales and dolphins.


Day 16-17: Sinharaja Rainforest Reserve

  • Guided trek in Sinharaja, a biodiversity hotspot.


Day 18: Galle and Departure

  • Explore Galle Fort before heading to Colombo for your return flight.


Key Tips:

  • Best Time: November to April for most parks.
  • Packing: Binoculars, camera with zoom, and insect repellent.
  • Guides: Always hire local experts for tracking and safety.


This signature itinerary ensures a deep immersion into Sri Lankas rich natural heritage, focusing on conservation and authentic wildlife encounters.
