Solo Female Signature Itinerary

Expertly crafted 12-day journey for the independent spirit

Back to Journey Hub

Safe Exploration Spotlight

For a perfect solo journey, keep these critical safety points in mind:

Train Safety

Avoid hanging out of doors. Stay inside for photos, especially near bridges.

Photo Etiquette

Never turn your back to Buddha statues. Be cautious on cliff edges in Ella.

Water Hazards

Waterfall rocks are slippery even if dry. Never swim alone in remote pools.

Sri Lanka is a fantastic destination for female solo travelers looking for a mix of culture, adventure, and relaxation. It is generally safe, offers great hospitality, and has well-connected transportation. This comprehensive 12-day signature itinerary is tailored for a female solo traveler, focusing on diverse experiences, safety, and convenience, while ensuring you get to enjoy the best of what Sri Lanka has to offer.

Duration: 12 Days

Best Time to Visit:
• West & South Coast: December to April
• East Coast: May to September

Day 1: Arrival in Colombo (Bandaranaike International Airport - CMB)

  • Arrive at Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) and take a pre-arranged airport taxi to your hotel in Colombo (1-hour drive).
  • Check into a reputable hotel or hostel in Colombo for safety and convenience.
  • Explore the city by visiting the Colombo National Museum and the Independence Memorial Hall.
  • Stroll along the Galle Face Green promenade in the evening and enjoy the sunset.

Stay: Colombo (1 night)

Day 2: Colombo to Kandy

  • Early morning train or car ride to Kandy (around 3 hours).
  • Visit the Temple of the Tooth Relic, an important Buddhist pilgrimage site.
  • Explore Kandy's local markets for souvenirs and local products.
  • Relax with a traditional Ayurvedic spa treatment in Kandy.

Stay: Kandy (1 night)

Day 3: Kandy to Sigiriya via Dambulla

  • Depart for Dambulla (2 hours) and visit the Cave Temple.
  • Continue to Sigiriya (30 minutes) and check into your guesthouse.
  • In the late afternoon, visit Pidurangala Rock for a scenic view of the Sigiriya Rock Fortress.

Stay: Sigiriya (1 night)

Day 4: Sigiriya & Polonnaruwa Day Trip

  • Early morning visit to the Sigiriya Rock Fortress (Lion Rock).
  • Head to Polonnaruwa (1 hour), an ancient city with beautiful ruins.
  • Rent a bicycle to explore the archaeological sites at your own pace.
  • Return to Sigiriya for a relaxed evening.

Stay: Sigiriya (1 night)

Day 5: Sigiriya to Nuwara Eliya

  • Leave for the hill town of Nuwara Eliya, also known as "Little England" (4-5 hours drive).
  • Take a tour of a local tea plantation and factory.
  • Enjoy a high tea experience at a classic tea venue.

Stay: Nuwara Eliya (1 night)

Day 6: Nuwara Eliya to Ella by Train

  • Take the iconic train ride from Nuwara Eliya to Ella (around 4 hours).
  • Upon arrival, check into a cozy guesthouse or boutique hostel.
  • Walk to Nine Arches Bridge for sunset.

Day 7: Explore Ella

  • Early morning hike up Little Adam's Peak for sunrise.
  • Visit the Ravana Falls.
  • Take a cooking class at the Ella Spice Garden.

Stay: Ella (2 nights)

Day 8: Ella to Yala National Park

  • Depart for Yala National Park (about 3 hours drive).
  • Join an evening safari to spot leopards, elephants, and other wildlife.

Stay: Yala (1 night)

Day 9: Yala to Tangalle

  • Early morning safari (optional).
  • Depart for Tangalle (around 3 hours drive), a peaceful southern beach town.
  • Spend the afternoon relaxing on Tangalle Beach or visiting Rekawa turtle conservation projects.

Stay: Tangalle (1 night)

Day 10: Tangalle to Mirissa

  • Drive to Mirissa (1.5 hours drive).
  • Spend the day relaxing or exploring the nearby Secret Beach.
  • Enjoy fresh seafood dinner at a beachside restaurant.

Day 11: Mirissa & Whale Watching

  • Early morning whale watching tour (seasonal from November to April).
  • Explore the shops and cafes in Mirissa town.
  • Relax with a yoga class or spa session in the evening.

Stay: Mirissa (2 nights)

Day 12: Mirissa to Galle & Colombo (Departure)

Expressway Tip: Use the Southern Expressway (E01) for the final leg from Galle back to Colombo. It reduces travel time to under 2 hours, making your airport transfer much smoother and predictable.

  • Head to the historic city of Galle (1-hour drive).
  • Explore the Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Continue to Colombo and transfer to the airport for your departure.

Safety and Practical Tips for Female Solo Travelers

  • Accommodation: Choose reputable hotels or hostels that are well-reviewed by other female travelers. Areas like Colombo, Kandy, and Ella have many female-friendly accommodation options.
  • Transportation: Use official taxis or rideshare services like PickMe or Uber in cities. Trains and buses are safe, but consider booking a private driver for longer journeys.
  • Clothing: Sri Lanka is a conservative country. Dress modestly when visiting temples or rural areas. Light, breathable clothing is recommended due to the tropical climate.
  • Local SIM Card: Purchase a local SIM card (Dialog or Mobitel) for internet access and safety. Always have emergency contacts handy.
  • Health & Hygiene: Carry a personal first aid kit, hand sanitizer, and tissue paper. Drink bottled water and eat at reputable restaurants.
  • interaction with Locals: Sri Lankans are friendly and hospitable. However, if you feel uncomfortable, remain firm but polite. Its fine to ignore unwanted attention and maintain boundaries.
  • Emergency Contacts: Save local emergency numbers and keep the contact details of your accommodation and travel guide (if you have one).
  • Joining Tours & Activities: Join group tours or activities like cooking classes, yoga retreats, or guided hikes. Its a great way to meet other travelers and stay safe.
