Solo Female Travel Plan

Expertly crafted 10-day itinerary

Back to All Plans

Sri Lanka is a fantastic destination for female solo travelers looking for a mix of culture, adventure, and relaxation. It’s generally safe, offers great hospitality, and has well-connected transportation. This comprehensive 12-day itinerary is tailored for a female solo traveler, focusing on diverse experiences, safety, and convenience, while ensuring you get to enjoy the best of what Sri Lanka has to offer.

Duration: 12 Days  

Best Time to Visit: December to March (for the west and south coasts) or April to September (for the east coast)


Day 1: Arrival in Colombo

  • Arrive at Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) and take a pre-arranged airport taxi to your hotel in Colombo (1-hour drive).
  • Check into a reputable hotel or hostel in Colombo for safety and convenience.
  • Explore the city by visiting the Colombo National Museum and the Independence Memorial Hall.
  • Stroll along the Galle Face Green promenade in the evening and enjoy the sunset.
  • Visit the Dutch Hospital Shopping Precinct for boutique shopping and a relaxing dinner.

Stay: Colombo (1 night)


Day 2: Colombo to Kandy

  • Early morning train or car ride to Kandy (around 3 hours).
  • Check into a hotel or guesthouse near Kandy Lake.
  • Visit the Temple of the Tooth Relic, an important Buddhist pilgrimage site.
  • Explore Kandy’s local markets, especially the Kandy City Center, for souvenirs and local products.
  • Relax with a traditional Ayurvedic spa treatment in one of Kandy’s reputable spas.

Stay: Kandy (1 night)


Day 3: Kandy to Dambulla & Sigiriya

  • Depart for Dambulla (2 hours drive) and visit the Dambulla Cave Temple, known for its well-preserved Buddhist murals and statues.
  • Continue to Sigiriya (30 minutes) and check into a guesthouse or eco-lodge.
  • In the late afternoon, visit Pidurangala Rock for a scenic view of the Sigiriya Rock Fortress and the surrounding forests.
  • Enjoy a peaceful evening in Sigiriya with a farm-to-table dinner at a local restaurant.

Stay: Sigiriya (1 night)


Day 4: Sigiriya to Polonnaruwa Day Trip

  • Early morning visit to the Sigiriya Rock Fortress (Lion Rock). Take your time exploring the ancient ruins and the breathtaking views from the top.
  • Head to Polonnaruwa (1-hour drive), an ancient city with beautiful ruins, palaces, and stupas.
  • Rent a bicycle to explore Polonnaruwa’s archaeological sites at your own pace.
  • Return to Sigiriya for a relaxed evening and possibly a local cultural show.

Stay: Sigiriya (1 night)


Day 5: Sigiriya to Nuwara Eliya

  • Leave for the hill town of Nuwara Eliya, also known as “Little England” (4-5 hours drive).
  • Check into a charming colonial-style hotel or guesthouse.
  • Take a tour of a local tea plantation and factory to learn about Sri Lanka’s world-famous Ceylon tea.
  • Enjoy a high tea experience at the **Grand Hotel** or another classic tea venue.

Stay: Nuwara Eliya (1 night)


Day 6: Nuwara Eliya to Ella by Train

  • Take the iconic train ride from Nuwara Eliya to Ella, which is known for its stunning views of tea plantations, lush forests, and waterfalls (around 4 hours).
  • Upon arrival in Ella, check into a cozy guesthouse or boutique hostel.
  • Walk to Nine Arches Bridge and admire the view of this stunning architectural wonder.
  • Spend the evening at Chill Café or one of the many relaxed cafes in Ella.

Day 7: Explore Ella

  • Early morning hike up Little Adam’s Peak for a sunrise view. The hike is short and offers a beautiful panoramic view of the surrounding hills.
  • Visit the Ravana Falls and enjoy some time by the water.
  • Take a cooking class at the Ella Spice Garden and learn to prepare traditional Sri Lankan dishes.
  • Spend the evening relaxing at one of the many wellness centers for yoga or meditation.

Stay: Ella (2 nights)


Day 8: Ella to Yala National Park

  • Depart for Yala National Park (about 3 hours drive).
  • Check into a safari lodge or eco-friendly campsite near Yala.
  • Join an evening safari to spot leopards, elephants, and other wildlife.
  • Enjoy dinner under the stars, and opt for a group bonfire or storytelling session organized by the lodge.

Stay: Yala (1 night)


Day 9: Yala to Tangalle

  • Early morning safari (if desired) for more wildlife sightings.
  • Depart for Tangalle, a peaceful beach town on the southern coast (around 3 hours drive).
  • Check into a beachside guesthouse or boutique hotel.
  • Spend the afternoon relaxing on Tangalle Beach or visiting Rekawa Beach to observe turtle conservation projects.

Stay: Tangalle (1 night)


Day 10: Tangalle to Mirissa

  • Drive to Mirissa (1.5 hours drive).
  • Check into a beachside guesthouse or hotel.
  • Spend the day relaxing on Mirissa Beach.
  • Explore the nearby Secret Beach, a secluded cove perfect for a peaceful swim and sunbathing.
  • End the day with dinner at a beachside restaurant, enjoying fresh seafood.

Day 11: Mirissa & Whale Watching

- Early morning whale watching tour (best from November to April).

- After the tour, explore the shops and cafes in Mirissa.

- Head to **Weligama Beach** for a beginner’s surfing lesson or just enjoy the lively atmosphere.

- In the evening, unwind with a yoga class or spa session in Mirissa.

Stay: Mirissa (2 nights)


Day 12: Mirissa to Galle & Colombo

  • Head to the historic city of Galle (1-hour drive).
  • Explore the Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site filled with charming cafes, boutique shops, and colonial architecture.
  • Visit Flag Rock and the Dutch Reformed Church, and take a walk along the fort walls for stunning ocean views.
  • Continue your journey to Colombo (2 hours drive).
  • Spend your final day shopping at places like Barefoot and Odel, and visit Viharamahadevi Park for some relaxation.
  • In the evening, transfer to the airport for your departure.


Safety and Practical Tips for Female Solo Travelers

  • Accommodation: Choose reputable hotels or hostels that are well-reviewed by other female travelers. Areas like Colombo, Kandy, and Ella have many female-friendly accommodation options.
  • Transportation: Use official taxis or rideshare services like PickMe or Uber in cities. Trains and buses are safe, but consider booking a private driver for longer journeys.
  • Clothing: Sri Lanka is a conservative country. Dress modestly when visiting temples or rural areas. Light, breathable clothing is recommended due to the tropical climate.
  • Local SIM Card: Purchase a local SIM card (Dialog or Mobitel) for internet access and safety. Always have emergency contacts handy.
  • Health & Hygiene: Carry a personal first aid kit, hand sanitizer, and tissue paper. Drink bottled water and eat at reputable restaurants.
  • interaction with Locals: Sri Lankans are friendly and hospitable. However, if you feel uncomfortable, remain firm but polite. It’s fine to ignore unwanted attention and maintain boundaries.
  • Emergency Contacts: Save local emergency numbers and keep the contact details of your accommodation and travel guide (if you have one).
  • Joining Tours & Activities: Join group tours or activities like cooking classes, yoga retreats, or guided hikes. It’s a great way to meet other travelers and stay safe.


This itinerary ensures a diverse experience that combines adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion while keeping safety and convenience in mind for female solo travelers. Feel free to adjust based on your preferences and interests!
