Sea Surfing Signature Itinerary

Expertly crafted 10-day journey for ocean addicts

Back to Journey Hub

Surfer Safety Spotlight

For a perfect and safe surf session, keep these local coastal rules in mind:

Rip Currents

Be aware of strong rip currents in Hikkaduwa and Arugam Bay. Always surf with a buddy or near a lifeguard station.

Reef Hazards

Reef breaks can be shallow and sharp. Wear booties if surfing at Rams or Benny's to prevent coral cuts.

Tropical Sun

The Sri Lankan sun is intense. Use reef-safe ZINC or high SPF sunblock and stay hydrated between sessions.

Sri Lanka is a hidden gem for surfers, offering pristine beaches, consistent waves, and warm waters. With a variety of surf spots catering to beginners, intermediate, and advanced surfers, it is an ideal destination for a surf tour. This guide provides a comprehensive signature itinerary for a 10-day surf experience in Sri Lanka, covering the best spots, accommodation, and local vibes.

Pro Tip: Choosing the Right Coast

Sri Lanka has two distinct surf seasons. For a 10-day trip, pick one coast based on your travel dates to avoid flat waters or long cross-country drives:

  • November – April: Stick to the Southwest Coast (Hikkaduwa, Weligama, Mirissa).
  • May – September: Head straight to the East Coast (Arugam Bay).

Duration: 10 Days

Day 1-3: Arrival in Colombo (Bandaranaike International Airport - CMB) and Surfing in Hikkaduwa

  • Location: Southwest Coast
  • Best Time: November to April
  • Surf Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Surf Spots:

  1. Main Reef: Consistent right-handers for beginners and intermediate surfers.
  2. Bennys: A reef break for more experienced surfers.

Day 4-5: Surfing in Weligama

  • Location: South Coast
  • Best Time: November to April
  • Surf Level: Beginner

Surf Spots:

  1. Weligama Beach Break: Great for beginners with small, consistent waves.
  2. Lazy Left: A mellow left-hander suitable for longboarders.

Day 6-7: Surfing in Midigama and Ahangama

  • Location: South Coast
  • Best Time: November to April
  • Surf Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Surf Spots:

  1. Rams: A powerful reef break with a fast right-hander.
  2. Lazy Right: A relaxed wave ideal for intermediate surfers.
  3. Coconut Point: A right-hander with long rides and fewer surfers.

Day 8-9: Surfing in Arugam Bay

Logistical Note: Moving from the South to Arugam Bay is a 6-hour drive. In one 10-day trip, we recommend choosing either the South or the East coast for the best experience.

  • Location: East Coast
  • Best Time: May to October

Surf Spots:

  1. Main Point: A famous right-hand point break.
  2. Peanut Farm: A quiet spot offering mellow waves.
  3. Whiskey Point: Ideal for dawn patrols.

Day 10: Relaxation and Departure

Spend the final day relaxing at a wellness retreat or enjoying a final surf session before heading back to Colombo for your departure (approx. 2.5 hours from South / 7 hours from East via Expressway).

Tips for a Successful Surf Voyage:

  • Transport: Consider renting a scooter or tuk-tuk with a surf rack for local movement.
  • Board Rental: Widely available at all major spots; no need to bring your own unless professional.
  • Accuracy: Surf conditions depend on sandbars and monsoon shifts; always check a local forecast.

"This tour plan offers a well-balanced experience, covering Sri Lanka’s most iconic surf destinations while ensuring a safe and exciting journey through the island’s coastal waters. It is designed as a flexible guide to help you plan your trip, and can be easily customized based on your interests, travel style, and available time."
