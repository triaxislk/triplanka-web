Sea Surfing Signature Itinerary

Expertly crafted 10-day journey for ocean addicts

Back to Journey Hub

Surfer Safety Spotlight

For a perfect and safe surf session, keep these local coastal rules in mind:

Rip Currents

Be aware of strong rip currents in Hikkaduwa and Arugam Bay. Always surf with a buddy or near a lifeguard station.

Reef Hazards

Reef breaks can be shallow and sharp. Wear booties if surfing at Rams or Benny's to prevent coral cuts.

Tropical Sun

The Sri Lankan sun is intense. Use reef-safe ZINC or high SPF sunblock and stay hydrated between sessions.

Sri Lanka is a hidden gem for surfers, offering pristine beaches, consistent waves, and warm waters. With a variety of surf spots catering to beginners, intermediate, and advanced surfers, its an ideal destination for a surf tour. This guide provides a comprehensive signature itinerary for a 10-day surf experience in Sri Lanka, covering the best spots, accommodation, and local vibes.

Duration: 10 Days

Day 1-3: Arrival and Surfing in Hikkaduwa

  • Location: Southwest Coast
  • Closest Airport: Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) in Colombo.
  • Best Time to Visit: November to April.
  • Surf Level: Beginner to Intermediate.

Surf Spots:

1. Main Reef: Consistent right-handers for beginners and intermediate surfers.

2. Bennys: A reef break for more experienced surfers.


Day 4-5: Surfing in Weligama

  • Location: South Coast
  • Best Time to Visit: November to April.
  • Surf Level: Beginner.

Surf Spots:

1. Weligama Beach Break: Great for beginners with small, consistent waves.

2. Lazy Left: A mellow left-hander suitable for longboarders.


Day 6-7: Surfing in Midigama and Ahangama

  • Location: South Coast
  • Best Time to Visit: November to April.
  • Surf Level: Intermediate to Advanced.

Surf Spots:

1. Rams: A powerful reef break with a fast right-hander, suited for advanced surfers.

2. Lazy Right: A relaxed wave ideal for intermediate surfers.

3. Coconut Point: A right-hander with long rides and fewer surfers.


Day 8-9: Surfing in Arugam Bay

  • Location: East Coast
  • Best Time to Visit: May to October.
  • Surf Level: Intermediate to Advanced.

Surf Spots:

1. Main Point: A famous right-hand point break for intermediate and advanced surfers.

  • 2. Peanut Farm: A quiet spot offering mellow waves and a beautiful setting.
  • 3. Whiskey Point: An ideal spot for dawn patrols with a picturesque view.


    • Day 10: Relaxation and Departure

    • Spend the final day relaxing at a wellness retreat or enjoying a final surf session before heading back to Colombo for your departure.


    Tips for a Successful Surf Voyage:

    • Transport: Consider renting a scooter or tuk-tuk to move between spots.
    • Surfboard Rental: Surfboards are widely available for rent at all major surf spots.
    • Local Culture: Respect local customs and be mindful of the environment.


    Sri Lankas surf culture is vibrant and growing, making it a top destination for an unforgettable surf tour. Enjoy the warm waters, picturesque landscapes, and friendly local culture as you explore the best of Sri Lankas surf scene.
