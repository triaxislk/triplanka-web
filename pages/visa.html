On 25 July 2025 the Sri Lankan government announced a visa‑free / visa‑fee waiver programme for 40 countries to support inbound tourism. The policy aims to simplify short tourist visits; however travellers should still complete the official Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) when required and confirm entry conditions for their nationality.

Quick summary: Citizens of the listed countries are generally eligible for a visa‑fee waiver / visa‑free entry into Sri Lanka. The standard permitted stay under this programme is up to 30 days for most categories — verify specific conditions for your passport and purpose of travel on the official ETA portal. Travellers must always check the ETA portal or Sri Lanka Department of Immigration & Emigration for the latest rules.

FAQ

Do travellers still need to apply for an ETA?

Yes — in most cases travellers should complete the ETA process even if they are eligible for a fee waiver. The ETA system records traveller details and is the official entry clearance mechanism.

How long can I stay if I'm from one of these countries?

Most sources and the initial announcements indicate a standard stay of up to 30 days. Some categories of traveller or passport types may have different entitlements; always check the ETA decision and the immigration stamp at arrival.

Is this list permanent?

No — this policy may be updated by Sri Lankan authorities. We recommend verifying the status close to travel and linking to official portals.
