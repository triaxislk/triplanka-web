Nuwara Eliya & Kitulgala

Emerald Tea Hills and the Thrill of the Rushing Rapids.

Nuwara Eliya: Little England

Known as "Little England, " Nuwara Eliya is a charming hill station nestled at the base of Pidurutalagala, Sri Lanka's tallest mountain. Its cool, misty climate and lush green landscapes are a refreshing departure from the coastal heat. The town still retains much of its colonial character, with British-style cottages, well-manicured gardens, and a classic golf course.

Surrounded by prime tea-growing regions, Nuwara Eliya offers visitors the chance to tour historic tea factories and witness the intricate process of creating the world-famous Ceylon Tea.

Kitulgala: The Adventure Hub

Photo by Charith Kodagoda

Nestled in a lush rainforest, Kitulgala is the adrenaline capital of Sri Lanka. It's most famous as the premier destination for white-water rafting on the Kelani River, with rapids suitable for both beginners and veterans.

Beyond the rapids, the region is a sanctuary for birdwatchers and nature lovers, offering jungle treks, canyoning, and waterfall abseiling in some of the island's most vibrant biodiversity.

Tea Estate Tours

Visit iconic estates like Pedro and Mackwoods to learn about the history and craftsmanship of tea production.

White-Water Rafting

Experience the thrill of the Kelani River rapids in Kitulgala, an essential adventure for every thrill-seeker.

Horton Plains

A short drive away, this plateau offers stunning treks to 'World's End' and the beautiful Baker's Falls.

Highland Adventure Tips

Cooler Climate

Temperatures in Nuwara Eliya can drop significantly at night. Always carry warm clothing and a light jacket.

Stay Waterproof

Kitulgala is in the wet zone. Carry waterproof bags for your gear and be prepared for sudden rain during jungle treks.

Sturdy Footwear

Whether you're trekking in Horton Plains or navigating the riverbanks in Kitulgala, good hiking shoes or water shoes are essential.
