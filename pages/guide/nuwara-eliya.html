Nuwara Eliya & Kitulgala

Mist-covered Mountains, Tea Estates, and Thrilling Adventures.

Nuwara Eliya: Little England

Set at an altitude of 1,868 meters, Nuwara Eliya is often referred to as "Little England" due to its cool climate, Tudor-style bungalows, and well-manicured gardens. Founded in the 19th century by Sir Samuel Baker, it became a beloved retreat for British colonists seeking to escape the tropical heat.

Today, it remains the heart of Sri Lanka's tea country, surrounded by sprawling estates that produce some of the world's finest Ceylon tea. The mist-covered peaks and cascading waterfalls like Lover's Leap offer a serene escape into nature.

Tea Heritage

Visit iconic estates like Mackwoods or Pedro Tea Factory to learn the "leaf to cup" process and enjoy a fresh tasting.

Gregory Lake

A tranquil spot for boating, swan-shaped pedal rides, and lakeside relaxation in the heart of the town.

Horton Plains

Trek to "World's End," a dramatic 880-meter drop offering one of the most breathtaking views in the country.

Kitulgala: The Adventure Hub

Kitulgala is Sri Lanka's premier destination for adrenaline seekers. Famous for white-water rafting on the Kelani River, it offers thrills for both beginners and experts. The town also served as the filming location for the Oscar-winning *The Bridge on the River Kwai*.

Beyond the rapids, explore the prehistoric Belilena Cave, which has housed human settlements for over 32,000 years, or go trekking through the lush Makandawa Rainforest Reserve.

  • Waterfall Abseiling
  • Jungle Trekking
  • Bird Watching

Highland Travel Tips

Pack Warmly

Evenings in Nuwara Eliya can be quite chilly. Bring sweaters, jackets, and long trousers.

The Train Journey

The route between Kandy and Ella (via Nanu Oya) is considered one of the world's most beautiful.

Dry Season

December to April is the best time for dry weather and clear views at World's End.
