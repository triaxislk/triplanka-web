Ratnapura & Sri Pada

The Glistening City of Gems and the Sacred Peak of the Gods.

Ratnapura: The Gem Capital

Ratnapura, which translates to "City of Gems, " is the world-renowned center of Sri Lanka's gem industry. For centuries, it has produced some of the finest sapphires, rubies, and emeralds in existence. The city is nestled in a lush, fertile valley surrounded by tea and rubber plantations, offering a unique blend of industrial heritage and natural beauty.

Beyond the gem mines, Ratnapura serves as a gateway to the Saman Devalaya, a historic temple dedicated to the deity Saman, the guardian of the region and Adam's Peak.

Photo by Sanjeew Sanjaya

Adam's Peak (Sri Pada)

Photo by Dilen Arunodya

The majestic Adam's Peak, or Sri Pada, is a sacred pilgrimage site for four major religions. At its summit lies a footprint believed by Buddhists to be the Buddha's, by Hindus to be Shiva's, and by Christians and Muslims to be Adam's or St. Thomas's.

The ascent, particularly during the pilgrimage season (December to May), is a profound spiritual journey. Witnessing the sunrise from the summit, with the triangular shadow of the peak projected onto the morning mist, is a truly life-changing experience.

Gem Mining & Museums

Observe traditional pit mining techniques and visit local museums to see rare gems and learn about their history.

Saman Devalaya

A stunning example of Kandyan architecture and a centerpiece of the region's spiritual and cultural life.

Bopath Ella Waterfall

A beautiful waterfall named for its resemblance to the leaf of the sacred Bo Tree, perfect for a relaxing day trip.

Gem-Country Travel Tips

Rainy Region

Ratnapura is one of the wettest regions in Sri Lanka. Always carry an umbrella or raincoat, regardless of the season.

Gem Tours

When visiting gem mines, always go with a reputable guide to ensure safety and ethical practices.

Adam's Peak

If starting your Sri Pada pilgrimage from Ratnapura, be prepared for a more rugged and longer ascent than the Hatton route.
