Ratnapura & Avissawella

Emerald Valleys, Sparkling Gems, and Ancient Echoes.

Ratnapura: The City of Gems

Nestled at the foot of the central highlands, Ratnapura is world-famous as the "City of Gems." For over two millennia, this region has been the source of some of the world's most exquisite sapphires, rubies, and alexandrites, which have graced the crowns of royalty across the globe.

Beyond the mines, Ratnapura is a gateway to the sacred Sri Pada (Adam's Peak) and the ancient Maha Saman Devalaya. It is a land of lush rubber and tea plantations, where mist-covered mountains provide a stunning backdrop to a thriving gem-trading community.

Avissawella: The Gateway to the Highlands

Avissawella, historically known as Seethawakapura, was once the heart of the powerful Seethawaka Kingdom. Today, it serves as a picturesque transit point where the lowlands meet the hills.

Explore the lush Seethawaka Botanical Garden, a sanctuary for rare wet-zone flora, or journey into the prehistoric past at Batadomba Lena, an archaeological site revealing traces of early human life dating back 8,000 years.

Gem Pit Tours

Experience the traditional art of gem mining, where miners use age-old techniques to uncover earth's treasures.

Sinharaja Rainforest

Trek through a UNESCO World Heritage site and biodiversity hotspot, home to rare endemic birds and ancient trees.

Cascading Falls

Discover the leaf-shaped Bopath Ella and the stunning Katugas Ella waterfalls tucked away in the jungle.

Rathnapura & Avissawella Tips

Gem Buying

Only buy from NGJA licensed dealers. Always request a certificate of authenticity for high-value stones.

Wet Zone Weather

These regions receive significant rainfall. The best time for trekking is during the drier months of January to April.

Adam's Peak

If starting your Sri Pada pilgrimage from Ratnapura, be prepared for a more rugged and longer ascent than the Hatton route.
