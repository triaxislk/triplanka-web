Anuradhapura & Mihintale

The Sacred Birthplace of Civilization and Spiritual Enlightenment.

Anuradhapura: The First Kingdom

Anuradhapura, the first royal capital of Sri Lanka, was established in the 4th century BC and remained the seat of power for over a thousand years. It is a city where every stone tells a story of ancient engineering, spiritual devotion, and artistic brilliance. Its sprawling complex of ruins, massive stupas, and sacred groves make it one of the most significant archaeological sites in the world.

The city's heartbeat is the Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi, the oldest documented tree in the world, grown from a sapling of the original Bodhi tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment.

Photo by Sanjeew Sanjaya

Mihintale: The Cradle of Buddhism

Photo by Dimath Dayananda

Near Anuradhapura lies Mihintale, a mountain peak believed to be the meeting place between the Buddhist monk Mahinda and King Devanampiyatissa, which inaugurated the presence of Buddhism in Sri Lanka.

Climb the 1, 840 ancient stone steps to the summit for panoramic views of the surrounding plains and to explore sacred sites like the Ambasthala Dagaba.

Ruwanwelisaya

A marvel of ancient architecture, this massive white stupa is one of the world's tallest monuments.

Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi

The sacred Bodhi tree, a living link to the Buddha's enlightenment, worshipped for over 2, 200 years.

Kuttam Pokuna

The "Twin Ponds," a masterpiece of ancient Sri Lankan landscape architecture and hydrological engineering.

Pilgrimage Travel Tips

Sacred Dress Code

White or light-colored modest clothing (covering shoulders and knees) is preferred for visiting sacred sites.

Barefoot Respect

You must remove shoes when entering temple grounds and certain archaeological areas.

Sun Protection

Exploring the vast ruins involves a lot of walking. Carry water and wear a hat (remove it when entering sacred zones).
