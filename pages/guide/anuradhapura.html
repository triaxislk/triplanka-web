Anuradhapura & Yapahuwa

The Cradle of Civilization and Forgotten Rock Citadels.

Anuradhapura: The Sacred Capital

Anuradhapura was the first capital of ancient Sri Lanka and the heart of Sinhalese civilization for over a millennium. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to some of the most venerated Buddhist monuments in the world.

Experience the spiritual power of the Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi, the oldest living human-planted tree in the world, or marvel at the scale of Ruwanwelisaya and Jetavanaramaya, which were once among the tallest structures in the ancient world.

Yapahuwa: The Medieval Citadel

Rising 90 meters above the plains, Yapahuwa served as the capital of Sri Lanka in the 13th century. It is most famous for its stunning ornamental stone staircase, which led to the temple that once housed the Sacred Tooth Relic.

The intricate carvings and dramatic location make it one of the island's most photogenic and historically significant military outposts.

Isurumuniya

A rock temple famous for its intricate stone carvings, including the "Isurumuniya Lovers."

Sri Maha Bodhi

A sacred fig tree grown from a cutting of the original Bodhi Tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment.

Mihintale

Known as the cradle of Buddhism in Sri Lanka, where the monk Mahinda met King Devanampiya Tissa.
