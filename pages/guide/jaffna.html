Explore Jaffna

The Authentic Heart of Hindu Culture and Resilient Spirit.

Introduction

Jaffna, located at the northernmost tip of Sri Lanka, is a city of distinct character, shaped by its deep Tamil heritage and resilient spirit. It offers a fascinating contrast to the rest of the island, with its vibrant Hindu temples, colonial architecture, and pristine islands.

After years of isolation, Jaffna has re-emerged as a captivating destination. Its palm-fringed landscapes, turquoise lagoons, and the warmth of its people make it an essential stop for those seeking an authentic Sri Lankan experience beyond the usual tourist trails.

Must-Visit Sites

Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil

The most iconic Hindu temple in Jaffna, renowned for its golden gopuram and vibrant festivals.

Jaffna Fort

The second-largest colonial fort in Sri Lanka, originally built by the Portuguese and later expanded by the Dutch.

Jaffna Public Library

A symbol of the city's intellectual heritage and a beautiful example of neo-Mughal architecture.

The Pristine Islands

Embark on a journey to the remote islands of the Jaffna peninsula. Nainativu is home to the sacred Nagadeepa Purana Viharaya and the Nagapooshani Amman Kovil.

For a truly off-the-beaten-path experience, visit Delft Island, known for its wild horses and coral-stone walls, a unique legacy of Dutch colonial influence.

Travel Tips for the North

Taste the North

Don't miss the famous Jaffna Crab Curry and the unique local ice cream at Rio Ice Cream.

Temple Etiquette

When visiting Hindu temples, men are often required to remove their shirts, and modesty in dress is essential.

Best Time to Visit

January to September offers the best weather for exploring the northern islands and temples.
