Mannar & The Islands

Ancient Bridges, Rare Wildlife, and Untouched Coastal Serenity.

Mannar: A Land of Myth and History

Mannar, located on the northwestern tip of Sri Lanka, is a place where history, myth, and nature collide. Once a thriving maritime hub for traders from Arabia to Greece, it remains one of the island's most unique and less-traveled destinations.

The island is famous for Adam's Bridge (Rama Setu), a chain of limestone shoals that historically connected Sri Lanka to India. Explore the Mannar Fort, a witness to Portuguese and Dutch colonial legacies, or stand beneath the ancient Baobab Tree, believed to have been brought by Arab traders centuries ago.

A Birdwatcher's Paradise

Mannar's semi-arid landscape, vast lagoons, and salt flats make it one of the richest biodiversity hotspots in South Asia. The Vankalai Sanctuary is a haven for migratory birds, including flamingos, pelicans, and spoonbills, which flock to the island during the winter months.

The Gulf of Mannar is also home to endangered dugongs and vibrant coral reefs, making it a critical area for marine conservation.

Mannar Fort

Visit the square-shaped fort built by the Portuguese in 1560 and later expanded by the Dutch.

Baobab Tree

Marvel at the "Bio-Tree," a massive 700-year-old landmark with a circumference of nearly 20 meters.

Thiruketheeswaram

Visit one of the five ancient 'Ishwarams' (Hindu temples) dedicated to Lord Shiva in Sri Lanka.

Mannar Travel Tips

Birdwatching

December to April is the peak season for migratory birds. Bring a good pair of binoculars.

Remote Travel

Mannar is relatively remote. Ensure you have a reliable driver or a private vehicle for exploring the island.

Modest Dress

Mannar has a mix of religious communities; modest attire is appreciated when visiting temples and mosques.
